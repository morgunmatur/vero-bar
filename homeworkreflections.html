Reflections regarding homework:

August 25th: Upon the creation of this first index, I didn't know of the wild ride I would be in for. I've known for a while that HTML was the most simple and popular to
learn first, but I didn't realize it could be so hard itself. Trying to assemble and work from the inside out in some cases while formatting a little website
is surprisingly challenging, as the barebones require more structure than I realized. It's so different than anything I've ever done. However, after trying
two different sites to get my first website up and running, I'm slowly but surely getting the hang of it.

September 1st: Taking and editing the two pictures were strangely nostalgic and reminded me of small details that I hadn't thought of in a long time. First, with the
cityscape, I actually took it from the 15th floor bathroom, which made me feel a little strange but happy to have captured something so cool in an
unassuming place. For me, it was amazing to have such a view from a simple bathroom, there was nothing like that in Florida. From flat land to 15+ stories
in the air on any given day, it's a great and interesting jump in perspectives. Second, the stairs, particularly the numbers and the levels in general
caught my attention. From 13 to 14 years old it felt like being bewteeen a rock and a hard place, never really sure if I was safe or ever emotionally
healthy. However, the stairs seemed to be a reference that there's nowhere else I can go but up, and I'm so looking forward to that everyday.
