My name is Veronica B, and I've lived in New York for two years since this past August.
Growing up in South Florida, I was exposed to many different cultures than included mostly
my own (Colombian), but also South/Central American and Caribbean. Thus, the art and influences
from those countries were all I knew for years. Broward County is not majority affluent at all, so
my peers and I grew up only hearing and imagining crowded cities with huge skyscrapers and immense
amounts of different foods you could have everyday for the rest of your life and never try twice.
Anywhere outside of the South was magical realism, an escape from what we knew. In response to our
homework prompts, I wanted to include images and words that reflect and remind me of the experience
of growing up on that weirdly isolated peninsula, and try to develop an understanding as to how it
shaped me and why I at times miss it so much.
