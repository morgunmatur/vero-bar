  1. On Photography, by Susan Sontag.

    2. Susan Sontag's words were exceptional in explaining the way photography has been essentially exploited in different ways since its inception in the 1800's.
    The uses from the perverse to family bonding were unfortunately still relatable, especially in the times we find ourselves in now. The individualism
    of the United States is so isolating at times, and photography is how I attempt to preserve my memory. If I don't have someone to share an experience with,
    a camera is always there to help capture an "interpreted" reminder, a word Sontag uses to describe photography.
