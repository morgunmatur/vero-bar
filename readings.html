  1. On Photography, by Susan Sontag.

    2. Susan Sontag's words were exceptional in explaining the way photography has been essentially exploited in different ways since its inception in the 1800's.
    The uses from the perverse to family bonding were unfortunately still relatable, especially in the times we find ourselves in now. The individualism
    of the United States is so isolating at times, and photography is how I attempt to preserve my memory. If I don't have someone to share an experience with,
    a camera is always there to help capture an "interpreted" reminder, a word Sontag uses to describe photography. Back to individualism, it can spawn really
    cool and interesting pieces of art in many forms, but the isolation for me has resulted in a feeling of not fitting in. I realized that it was put into words
    when I first listened to a Blood Orange song called Jewelry in 2018. The woman in the intro says, "So, like, my favorite images are the ones where someone
    who isn't supposed to be there, Who's like in a space, a space where we were not ever welcomed in or we were not invited..." While that quote delves deeper
    into the rhetoric of Blood Orange, it was hard to not relate. What I want to do with the photos taken in this class is remember those feelings and work on
    them, understand from where they arise, and know that it's natural to not always feel neutral towards everyting.
